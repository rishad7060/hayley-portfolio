Use UK English consistently across every copy surface ("colour", "personalised", "favourite", "tailored"). First-person voice when Hayley is speaking directly ("Hi, I'm Hayley…"); otherwise second-person brand voice addressing the client ("your curls"). Recurring signature phrase is "look and feel fabulous" — use it sparingly but keep it as a thread across hero, services, and CTAs.

**Why:** The client is based in Shropshire UK and the brief (content.md) is written in that register. Deviating to US English or overly corporate phrasing breaks the warmth and credibility.

**How to apply:** When writing or editing headlines, CTAs, FAQ answers, service descriptions, newsletter copy, or testimonials, default to UK spelling and the conversational, curl-positive tone of the brief. Prefer "curl-positive", "curl science", "curl pattern", "wavy, curly, coily" terminology over generic "hair salon" language.
